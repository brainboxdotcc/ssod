The Assassins' Guild is among the oldest and most discreet organisations in Cryptillia. From its headquarters in the northern city of Cryptillius Centralius, it provides services that few governments publicly acknowledge using, yet many quietly rely upon.

Despite the name, assassination is only one aspect of the guild's trade. Its members are masters of infiltration, espionage, surveillance, sabotage, forgery, disguise, and the silent acquisition of information. They can make a person disappear, but more often they make secrets disappear first.

A guild operative may spend months living under an assumed identity, serving in a noble's household, travelling as a merchant, or working as a dock labourer before a contract reaches its conclusion. Success is measured not by the body left behind, but by how little evidence remains that the operative was ever there.

Guild training is relentless. Every recruit studies poisons, lock mechanisms, codes, observation, escape routes, and the art of passing unnoticed. Crossbows, concealed blades and throwing weapons are favoured not because of tradition, but because they are quiet, reliable and easily hidden. An assassin who must draw a sword has usually allowed the mission to go terribly wrong.

Contrary to popular imagination, guild operatives rarely wear distinctive clothing or hide beneath black cloaks. They dress to become forgettable. The finest assassin is the one a witness confidently swears was never present.

The guild maintains a reputation for political neutrality. Kingdoms, merchant houses, nobles, criminal syndicates and even temples have all purchased its services. Gold buys discretion. More gold buys silence. Information, however, commands the highest price of all.

Within Cryptillia, rumours claim the guild has agents embedded in every major city. Whether those rumours are true is impossible to determine. That uncertainty is precisely how the guild prefers it.

**Assassins begin the game able to store one additional critical strike, allowing them to unleash devastating attacks when the opportunity presents itself.**
